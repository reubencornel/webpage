1 Abstract

The paper deals with the essential part of software engineering. For futher details regarding the "essential" look at the Code complete section. Fred Brooks suggests the removing accidental properties/difficulties will not affect software productivity unless they are doing something terribly wrong.

2 Introduction

People expect there to be silver bullet that would help control the cost of the software projects. But the author does not see us finding that bullet soon. He suggests that a "disciplined, consistent effort to develop, propagate and exploit" a number of innovations that would yeild an "order-of-magnitude" improvement.

3 Essential difficulties

The nature of software makes the development of a "silver bullet" difficult. It would be wrong to compare this with computer hardware, because we cannot expect inventions analogous to transistors and VLSI in the software engineering.

He tries to break up the difficulties in the software world according to the principles of Aristotle. With this he comes up with two types of difficulties, namely essence - difficulties inherent to the nature of software and accidents - those difficulties that are not inherent.

The essence of the software entity is a construct of interlocking concepts: data sets, relationships between data items, algorithms and functions. The essence is abstract but at the same time it is highly precise and richly detailed.

Thus he concludes that most of the difficulties in building software to be the specification, design and testing of the construct. Rather than the actual labour of building and testing the fidelity of the representation.

3.1 Inherent properties of modern software systems

3.1.1 Complexity

Complexity in software arises due to the fact that no two parts are alike, for example we refactor code that is repeated into a single function. Therefore this means that, unlike other fields where scaling up means repeating the same pattern over and over again, in software it means increasing the number of states and their interactions that we have to deal with.

Software complexity unlike complexity in the sciences is its essence therefore we cannot abstract it. This complexity arises because of

  • Difficulties in communication between team members that could lead to defects.
  • Inablity/Lack of understanding of the states of the program and from that come unreliablity.
  • Complexity of functions because of complex function interfaces.
  • Structure that makes extending program difficult.
  • Structure that may constitute security trapdoors.

3.1.2 Conformity

Unlike the physicist who works with the faith that there may be some underlying laws to the phenomena he deals with. The software engineer does not have that luxury because the software that he writes has to deal with the complexity imposed by the human institutions that his software has to deal with.
